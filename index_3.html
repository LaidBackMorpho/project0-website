What Music Means to Me

Music is a huge part of my life. If I'm not producing it, I'm listening to it. One of the best properties of music is summed up in this quote by Hans Christian Andersen, "Where words fail, music speaks.‚Äù I believe that there is nothing on Earth more unique than music.

Making music that I enjoy listening to is extremely satisfying, and when others appreciate the music I create, it makes the experience all the more rewarding. The practically infinite number of sounds any one instrument can create provides a liberating experience for anyone wanting to express themselves.

Here are some methods of music production with some pros and cons:

Method Pros Cons
Playing an instrument The simplest way of making music, great for beginners. The sounds made are limited to that instrumnt's timbre and polyphony.
Using a Digital Audio Workspace (DAW) Access to loads of instruments, effects, mixing mastering tools; much more involved than playing an instrument. Previous musical experience is a must, UI may take some getting used to.
Using Modular Synthesis The sounds created are very unique, can generate heaps of inspiration. Modular synthesis has a steep (nearly 90°) learning curve, physical modules can get pricy*.

* There are virtual modular synthesizers available for free

Now, in the table above I only scratched the surface of the various advatages and disadvantages of the methods mentioned. The best way to get a feel for them is to try each out, if it's affordable, that is.
